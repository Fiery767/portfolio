In early 2018, I was given the opportunity to visit the Cisco Systems Headquarters in San Jose, CA. Here I was able to meet several industry leaders and learn about ongoing technology they are working on. Particularly interesting were the "smart cities" they were developing, which allowed lights and parking all over a city to be controlled with the click of a button. It proved just how much can be accomplished with technology, and how far technology has come since electricity was first discovered. This visit allowed me to see how a regular workplace functioned, and also allowed me to learn more about current technology. We also participated in an activity in which we wrote out the steps to a simple action such as making a sandwich or building a snowman, then several of our peers would try to expose flaws in the process, simulating how hackers think, and how those working in security counter them. It was an extremely interesting and enlightening experience, and definitely left me wanting to learn more about internet security.

Back in October, 2017, two speakers from BSK Associates came to speak to my class. BSK Associates is an engineering consulting firm based in Livermore, CA. They work in geotechnical, environmental, and materials engineering, and are an employee owned company. They spoke to us about different techniques they used in the field to analyze soil samples, as well as local projects they worked on, including a nearby water park called The Wave. I was inspired that they were able to work on such a large scale project despite being an independent firm. I was previously unaware that engineering firms such as this existed, and so meeting people who are out there in the workforce was very insightful. Several of their current employees are alumni from my school, which was an encouragement to me that they were able to find local work and that the program introduced them to industry contacts and gave them the opportunity to break into the engineering field.

This past November, we had a speaker come in who had gone through the ROTC program and was in the Air Force as an engineer working in intelligence operations. He spoke to us about how the ROTC works and the opportunity available for engineering students to have their tuition paid by the government if they enroll in the Air Force. Though I would be unable to go this route without first becoming a U.S. citizen and going through that process, it was interesting to see that this option is available. The presentation given showed me how there is a wide range of options available to engineers, including various careers and fields.

Throughout this school year, I have the pleasure of being mentored by Roddy Kwan, a retired engineering director from Qualcomm, as part of the engineering academy at my school. So far, he has taught me about how to define success based on what I value. I have also learned about exactly working in engineering entails, and the different opportunities that are available.
